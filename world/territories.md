# BL0CKS World File: Territories

**Instructions for Game Engine:** This file defines the 6 playable neighborhoods on the map. Use these descriptions, notable intersections, and income rules when narrating consequences or generating Event cards. 

## The Board: South Side Grid

There are exactly 6 neighborhoods. Control can shift between `YOU`, `RIVAL_[FACTION_NAME]`, `NEUTRAL`, or `CONTESTED`.

### 1. Woodlawn
- **Status in Act I:** Player's base of operations.
- **Vibe:** Historic but scarred. Vacant lots next to beautiful old greystones. The University of Chicago looms right next door, making real estate developers hungry and the streets tense.
- **Key Intersections:** 63rd & King Drive (The Corner), 63rd & Cottage Grove.
- **Engine rule:** Woodlawn is the player's stronghold. Losing Woodlawn means severe loyalty penalties for all Governors.

### 2. Englewood
- **Status in Act I:** Controlled by the Lords (Rival).
- **Vibe:** The hardest block on the map. Highly territorial, maze-like dead ends, heavy police presence. To push into Englewood requires sheer force.
- **Key Intersections:** 69th & Halsted, 59th & Racine.
- **Engine rule:** Attacks on Englewood have a higher chance of triggering Police Scanner events.

### 3. Chatham
- **Status in Act I:** Contested / Middle-class buffer zone.
- **Vibe:** Middle-class, older residents, neatly manicured lawns. The money flows through here cleanly. The gangs don't hold corners here—they operate out of legitimate storefronts and barbershops.
- **Key Intersections:** 79th & Cottage Grove, 87th & King Drive.
- **Engine rule:** Taxing Chatham yields high resources, but brings immediate heat from the Commission.

### 4. Auburn Gresham
- **Status in Act I:** Contested / Lords pushing in.
- **Vibe:** Brick bungalows, bustling commercial strips. A transit hub. Whoever controls the transit stops controls the flow of product.
- **Key Intersections:** 79th & Halsted, 79th & Ashland.
- **Engine rule:** Controlling Auburn Gresham allows the player to move horizontally across the map without penalty.

### 5. Roseland
- **Status in Act I:** Controlled by the Stones (Rival).
- **Vibe:** The far south. Isolated, insular, self-governing. They don't respect the politics of the northern blocks. Very difficult to infiltrate with spies.
- **Key Intersections:** 111th & State, 115th & Michigan.
- **Engine rule:** Intel cards spent on Roseland targets have a 50% chance of returning false or vague information.

### 6. Hyde Park
- **Status in Act I:** Neutral (Off-limits).
- **Vibe:** Wealthy, heavily gentrified, private university police. The money here is massive, but the heat is lethal. No gang claims it openly.
- **Key Intersections:** 53rd & Harper, 55th & Lake Park.
- **Engine rule:** You cannot claim Hyde Park with War cards. You can only claim it via political/broker People cards.

---

**Engine Directive:** When generating an Event card, randomly pick one of the active territories to anchor the narrative physically in the space (e.g., "Sirens screaming down 79th and Cottage...").
